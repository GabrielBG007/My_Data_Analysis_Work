Recent Work

Excel

Intermediate - Advanced Level

I've been using Excel pretty much since my school days, but things got more serious in college where I started to learn more about the main functions and also the best ways of visualizing data. What really made me break a sweat was the elaboration of my TFG, where I had to deal with more than 20.000 rows of data! (that was a lot for me, considering my basic level of Excel). I had to learn better ways of cleaning data and organizing it.

I completed a 3.5-hour Advanced Excel course, where I learned about dynamic arrays as well as functions like XLOOKUP,XMATCH,TWO-WAY-LOOKUPS, CHOOSE,SWITCH, and all types of rounding. I also completed a course about pivot tables and another one related with data cleaning (both were an 1 hour long).

SQL

Basic - Intermediate Level

During my academic career I never made use of SQL. When I started to search for the requirements for becoming a Data Analyst, I found out that having a decent knowledge of SQL was needed, that's why I completed two online SQL courses, one lasting 4 hours and the other 20 hours.

In these courses I learned about the creation of tables, data types, writing basic query using UPDATE, DELETE, GROUP BY, ORDER BY as well as some more complicated queries using JOINS,UNIONS, nested queries...

R Studio

Intermediate Level

R Studio was the one that really got me into the world of data analysis, especially the visualization part of it. I was suggested a library called "openair" because my TFG was related to the topic of Air Pollution.

Time went by and I found myself a bit limited with the functionality of this library, that's why I began to look out for more libraries ( a right choice). I got to know libraries like "tidyverse", "cowplot", "datarium" ... wich really helped me to take the next step in improving my graphic design and also structuring my datasets. The amount of libraries that you can find to visualize and clean your data is quite impressive.

Python

Basic Level

Once I had completed the SQL course, I began a 16-hour Python course. Right away, I knew that this one was going to be a little bit more challenging, and I was spot on.

Python was more technical and also way more immersed into the "programming world". Here I learned about WHILE LOOPS, FOR LOOPS, LISTS, DICTIONARIES,FUNCTIONS, EXCEPTIONS, REGULAR EXPRESSIONS,METHODS, PROPERTIES... (a lot!!). I also completed a shorter course about supervised and unsupervised machine learning (this one was even harder to follow along with).

Tableau and Power BI

Basic - Intermediate Level

I completed a total of 8 hours of courses for both Tableau and Power BI platforms. I don't know why I wasn't told about these kind of programs in school or college; building charts and creating dashboards were way easier and also more eye-catching. What I noticed is that Tableau is better with the visualization aspect than Power BI, but the latter one is better with modelling thanks to Power Query, which makes data cleaning look easy.
